  • $\displaystyle{ 2^3\cdot2^4= }$

    Hint Write out all the 2's being multiplied in $2^3$ and $2^4$.
    Hint $\left(2\cdot2\cdot2\right)\cdot\left(2\cdot2\cdot2\cdot2\right) =2\cdot2\cdot2\cdot2\cdot2\cdot2\cdot2$
    Show/Hide Solution $2^3\cdot2^4 ~\\ =(2\cdot2\cdot2)\cdot(2\cdot2\cdot2\cdot2) ~\\ =2\cdot2\cdot2\cdot2\cdot2\cdot2\cdot2 ~\\ =2^7$

  • $\displaystyle{ \frac{2^7}{2^3}= }$

    Hint Write out all the 2's being multiplied.
    Hint $\frac{2\cdot2\cdot2\cdot2\cdot2\cdot2\cdot2}{2\cdot2\cdot2} =2\cdot2\cdot2\cdot2$
    Show/Hide Solution $\frac{2^7}{2^3} ~\\ =\frac{2\cdot2\cdot2\cdot2\cdot2\cdot2\cdot2}{2\cdot2\cdot2} ~\\ =2\cdot2\cdot2\cdot2 ~\\ =2^4$

  • $\displaystyle{ 2^4\cdot3^4= }$

    Hint Write out $2^4$ and $3^4$.
    Hint $\left(2\cdot2\cdot2\cdot2\right)\cdot\left(3\cdot3\cdot3\cdot3\right) =\left(2\cdot3\right)\cdot\left(2\cdot3\right)\cdot\left(2\cdot3\right)\cdot\left(2\cdot3\right)$
    Show/Hide Solution $2^4\cdot3^4 ~\\ =\left(2\cdot2\cdot2\cdot2\right)\cdot\left(3\cdot3\cdot3\cdot3\right) ~\\ =\left(2\cdot3\right)\cdot\left(2\cdot3\right)\cdot\left(2\cdot3\right)\cdot\left(2\cdot3\right) ~\\ =\left(2\cdot3\right)^4 ~\\ =6^4$

  • $\displaystyle{ \frac{6^4}{3^4}= }$

    Hint Write out $6^4$ and $3^4$.
    Hint $\frac{6\cdot6\cdot6\cdot6}{3\cdot3\cdot3\cdot3} =\left(\frac{6}{3}\right)\cdot\left(\frac{6}{3}\right)\cdot\left(\frac{6}{3}\right)\cdot\left(\frac{6}{3}\right)$
    Show/Hide Solution $\frac{6^4}{3^4} ~\\ =\frac{6\cdot6\cdot6\cdot6}{3\cdot3\cdot3\cdot3} ~\\ =\left(\frac{6}{3}\right)\cdot\left(\frac{6}{3}\right)\cdot\left(\frac{6}{3}\right)\cdot\left(\frac{6}{3}\right) ~\\ =\left(\frac{6}{3}\right)^4 ~\\ =2^4$

  • $\displaystyle{ \left(5^2\right)^4= }$ (Exp form)

    Hint Write out $5^2$.
    Hint $\left(5\cdot5\right)^4 =\left(5\cdot5\right)\cdot\left(5\cdot5\right)\cdot\left(5\cdot5\right)\cdot\left(5\cdot5\right)$
    Show/Hide Solution $\left(5^2\right)^4 ~\\ =\left(5\cdot5\right)^4 ~\\ =\left(5\cdot5\right)\cdot\left(5\cdot5\right)\cdot\left(5\cdot5\right)\cdot\left(5\cdot5\right) ~\\ =5\cdot5\cdot5\cdot5\cdot5\cdot5\cdot5\cdot5 ~\\ =5^8$

  • $\displaystyle{ 10^1= }$

    Hint How many 10's are being multiplied together?
    Hint How many zeroes does 10^2 have when written as an integer? What about 10^3 and 10^4? How many zeroes do you think 10^1 has?
    Show/Hide Solution

    Here are several ways to think about this:

      1. There is only one 10 being multiplied by itself. So the answer is 10.
      2. $10^2=100$, which is a 1 followed by 2 zeroes. $10^3=1000$ (3 zeroes) and $10^4=10000$ (4 zeroes). Thus, $10^1$ should be a 1 followed by 1 zero.
      3. We know that $\frac{x^a}{x^b}=x^{a-b}$. (See #2 in Part 1). Therefore, $\frac{10^3}{10^2}=10^{3-2}=10^1$. We also know that $\frac{10^3}{10^2}=\frac{1000}{100}$. So, $10^1=10$.

  • $\displaystyle{ 10^0= }$

    Hint How many zeroes do $10^1$, $10^2$, $10^3$, and $10^4$ have? How many zeroes do you think 10^0 has?
    Hint Try using the exponent laws you've discovered from Part 1.
    Show/Hide Solution

    Here are a couple of ways to think about this:

      1. When $x$ is positive, $10^x$ is a 1 followed by $x$ zeroes. Continuing the pattern, $10^0$ is a 1 followed by 0 zeroes, which is just 1.
      2. We know that $\frac{x^a}{x^b}=x^{a-b}$. (See #2 in Part 1). Therefore, $\frac{10^1}{10^1}=10^{1-1}=10^0$. We also know that $\frac{10^1}{10^1}=10/10=1$. So, $10^0=1$.
