    What are exponents?

    Exponents are a way of writing repeated multiplication. To show you what I mean, consider this expression:

    $3+3+3+3+3+3$

    Not very pretty, is it? You (should have) learned that this can also be written as

    $3\cdot6$

    which is 3 added to itself 6 times. Similarly, if you have an expression like this:

    $3\cdot3\cdot3\cdot3\cdot3\cdot3$

    You can write it using an exponent as

    $3^6$

    (read as 3 to the power of 6, 3 to the 6th power, or 3 to the 6th).

    In this example, the "3" is called the base, and it is the number being multiplied by itself. The "6" is called the exponent, and it is the number of times that the base is multiplied by itself. This notation is called exponential form.
